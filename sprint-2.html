The Four Stages

01
On-Site Treatment
At the wind farm, end-of-life blades are mechanically dismantled. Hydraulic shears and diamond wire saws cut the composite structures into 1–2 metre segments for transport to the recycling facility.
Input
  • End-of-life wind turbine blades
Output
  • Cut blade segments (1–2 m)
02
Mechanical Shredding
Segments are ground into smaller particles by a mechanical grinder. The smaller particle size increases surface area, improves heat transfer, and ensures uniform thermal treatment downstream.
Input
  • Cut blade segments
Output
  • GFRP particles (1–20 mm)
03
Thermal Decomposition Reactor
The ground feed enters the reactor at 550–660 °C. The thermoset polymer (epoxy resin) decomposes into gases and char. E-glass fibers remain solid — well below their ~850 °C softening point. The reactor outlet carries three product streams.
Input
  • GFRP particles (1–20 mm)
Outputs
  • Glass fiber fraction (solid)
  • Char fraction (solid)
  • Gas fraction (volatile)
04
Integrated Material Separation
A single integrated cyclone-classifier tower replaces two separate separators. The upper cyclone section separates gases from solids (by phase). The lower classification section separates glass fibers from char (by density). Three recovered streams from one tower.
Input
  • Mixed reactor outlet (gas + glass + char)
Outputs (3 streams)
  • Gas — top of cyclone
  • Glass fibers — by density
  • Char — by density

Usage & Replacement

All three recovered streams have downstream value. Glass fiber partially replaces silica sand and clay in cement clinker production. Char substitutes coal or petroleum coke as alternative fuel. Combustible gas is recovered as process fuel, cutting external energy demand. The process diverts blade waste from landfill and substitutes virgin raw materials and fossil fuels.
